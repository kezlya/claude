# About Page - Version 4 (Vitaly's Final Version)

Your automation is making money. That part worked.

Now it breaks at 3 AM and you're the only one who can fix it. An enterprise client asked about your security posture and you had nothing to say. Your Make credits are burning through empty polling checks while you sleep. Your n8n instance is running on a single VPS with SQLite and you're afraid to touch it because the last update wiped everything.

These are not bugs. They're ceilings. And the fix isn't to throw away the tools that got you here. The fix is to upgrade how you use them - pick stronger tools where needed, lock down the weak points, and build the parts that no-code can't handle.

This community is for entrepreneurs who have already built something real with AI automation and are hitting that ceiling. Not beginners. Not people exploring whether AI is worth trying. People who have real clients, real revenue, and a stack that works until it doesn't. Whether you're building for your own business or for clients who depend on you - if your workflows feel fragile, your costs are climbing, or you know your setup won't survive the next growth spike, you're in the right place.

I'm Vitaly. Ex-Google, Ex-Microsoft, 20+ years shipping production systems. I help entrepreneurs and startups go from "it works" to "it works at scale, securely, and I can sleep at night." I identify bottlenecks, fix weak points in your pipelines, introduce better tools where they matter, and help you figure out what would actually move the needle for your revenue. When the time comes to hire your first engineer or build a team, I help with that too.

I'm not here to tell you to stop using AI agents or no-code tools. I'm here for the conversation you can't have anywhere else: what actually breaks at scale, what security looks like in practice, and what to do when no-code stops being enough.

The community is free and it is selective. Not everyone gets in.

If you apply, the questions are straightforward: tell me what you're running, how you debug it when it breaks, and who's depending on it. There are no wrong answers - but there are answers that show me you have real stakes in this.

Apply below. I read every one.
