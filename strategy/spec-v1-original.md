**Version:** 1.0

## Executive Summary

Agent Orchestrator is a Skool community for people who build products using AI agents. The community serves two complementary audiences: **frustrated technical builders** seeking mentorship and **non-technical entrepreneurs** seeking to build AI-powered products. Founded by Vitaly Kezlya, a VP of Engineering with 20 years of experience at Google, Microsoft, and AI startups.

**Core Value Proposition:** Learn to orchestrate AI agents to build enterprise-grade products fast - from someone who has done it at scale.

## 3. Target Audience

### Primary Audience A: Frustrated Technical Builders
- **Who:** Developers/engineers building AI products but hitting walls
- **Pain:** Know how to code but struggling with architecture, scale, or AI integration
- **Outcome:** Ship MVP, get unstuck, level up to senior/staff level
- **Value to them:** Mentorship from someone who's been there at Google/Microsoft scale

### Primary Audience B: Non-Technical Entrepreneurs
- **Who:** Business people with ideas and money, need technical guidance
- **Pain:** Want to build AI products but can't evaluate tech decisions
- **Outcome:** Get products built correctly without getting burned by bad tech choices
- **Value to them:** Trusted tech advisor + access to vetted builders

### Audience Interaction Model
Both audiences in ONE community:
- Builders get mentorship + access to potential clients
- Entrepreneurs get guidance + access to vetted builders
- Organic collaboration happens naturally
- Vitaly orchestrates but doesn't micromanage matchmaking

### Who We DON'T Want
- "Sassy people" - complainers, constant critics
- Snowflakes - can't handle direct feedback
- Politics-focused - not here to build, here to debate
- Pure lurkers in paid tier - must participate
- Idea-only people - must be willing to execute

## 5. Community Structure

### Skool Platform Features Usage

| Feature | Priority | How We'll Use It |
|---------|----------|------------------|
| Community Feed | HIGH | Main discussion hub, separate threads for builders vs entrepreneurs |
| Calendar/Events | HIGH | Weekly hot seat sessions, occasional live builds |
| Classroom | MEDIUM | Recorded sessions library, methodology documentation |
| Leaderboards | LOW | Gamification for engagement (implement when community stabilizes) |

### Member Roles

**Vitaly (Founder)**
- Weekly hot seat reviews (30 min)
- Occasional live build sessions
- Strategic posts in community
- Final approval on membership applications

**Team Co-Moderators (Remote Dev Team employees)**
- Answer technical questions in community
- Serve as topic experts (frontend, backend, mobile, cloud)
- Flag interesting conversations to Vitaly
- Initial screening of applications
- Compensation: Part of job initially, bonus/commission when community profitable

**Senior Members (Earned)**
- Long-term engaged members who help newcomers
- Recognition in community, potential hot seat priority
- Future: Weekly champions rotation

### Onboarding Flow

**Phase 1: 0-20 members**
- CTA: Book a call with Vitaly
- 15-minute intro call to personally vet and build relationship
- Understand their goals, assess fit

**Phase 2: 20-100 members**
- CTA: Apply to join
- Application form with strategic questions
- Vitaly reviews and approves/rejects
- Rejection email: "Not the right fit at this time"

**Phase 3: 100+ members**
- CTA: Join with onboarding questions
- Automated approval with quality questions
- Manual review only for edge cases

### Application Questions (Draft - to refine)
1. What are you currently building? (Tests skin in game)
2. What's your biggest technical or product blocker right now? (Shows self-awareness)
3. Are you a builder (technical) or entrepreneur (non-technical)? (Segments audience)
4. What do you hope to get from this community? (Identifies expectations)
5. How did you hear about Agent Orchestrator? (Tracks growth channels)
6. [Scenario] You strongly disagree with advice given in the community. What do you do? (Filters for drama)
7. LinkedIn profile URL (Social proof check)

## 10. Risk Mitigation

### Risk: Vitaly unavailable (travel, family, busy)
**Mitigation:**
- Bank 5 pre-recorded sessions
- Team moderators handle daily engagement
- Community-led sessions as backup

### Risk: Mixed audience doesn't gel
**Mitigation:**
- Separate discussion threads by audience type
- Clear onboarding about community composition
- Monitor and adjust if tension emerges

### Risk: Paid tier doesn't convert
**Mitigation:**
- Don't launch until demand signal (5 people ask)
- Test pricing with early conversations
- Agency backend provides revenue regardless

### Risk: Can't create business content for entrepreneurs
**Mitigation:**
- Separate project for content calendar development
- Frame technical content for business outcomes
- Partner with someone for business content if needed
