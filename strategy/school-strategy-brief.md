# school.com - Strategy Brief
**Owner:** Vitaly (Fractional CTO, 20+ yrs experience, Google/Microsoft background)  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Purpose:** AI agent context document - community + fractional CTO business plan

---

## 1. THE BUSINESS IN ONE PARAGRAPH

school.com is a premium AI & automation community targeting technical founders and entrepreneurs. It runs two tiers: a free community (organic growth funnel) and a paid community capped at 20 members at $250/month. The paid community is intentionally small because the owner cannot hold more than 20 client contexts in his head simultaneously. The primary purpose of the community is NOT to maximize membership revenue - it is to serve as a trust-building funnel for Fractional CTO retainer packages priced at $4K-$10K/month. The community runs with 3 moderators (Ed and 2 others) on a 70/30 revenue split. The owner keeps 70%. All fractional CTO revenue is excluded from the mod split entirely.

---

## 2. POSITIONING

- **Niche:** AI & automation for technical founders and entrepreneurs
- **NOT competing with:** Generic AI tool communities, no-code automation communities, "make money with AI" content creators
- **Competing AS:** A practitioner-led premium community - post-graduate level, for people who already know the basics and want production-grade depth
- **Price signal:** $250/month communicates serious, professional audience. Low price ($29-49) would attract wrong audience and undermine CTO funnel.
- **Competitive moat:** Owner has 20+ years real engineering experience, active CTO clients, hands-on Go/GCP/AI implementation. Most AI community owners are content creators, not practitioners.

## 4. MODERATOR PROGRAM

### Structure
- 3 moderators Year 1: Ed (lead mod) + 2 others
- Revenue split: 70% owner / 30% all mods combined
- Mod pool split equally among active mods (not by individual performance - avoids disputes)
- CTO retainer revenue: 0% to mods, explicitly stated in agreement

### 3-Phase Year 1 Program
**Phase 1 (Months 1-3) - Founding Period**
- No cash
- Perks: lifetime free paid community access, Founding Moderator title (permanent), priority marketplace placement, 0% commission on their own services
- Retroactive milestone bonuses earned now, paid in Phase 2

**Phase 2 (Months 4-6) - Launch Period**
- Cash activates once paid community hits 20 members
- MRR share (30% ÷ 3 mods) kicks in

**Phase 3 (Months 7-12) - Growth Period**
- Same structure, improved rates
- Year-end loyalty bonus: $200 one-time for completing 12 months

### Bonus Structure
| Trigger | Amount |
|---|---|
| Free community +50 members/month | $10/mod |
| Free community +100 members/month | $25/mod |
| Free community hits 100 members | $25/mod retroactive |
| Free community hits 250 members | $50/mod retroactive |
| Free community hits 500 members | $100/mod retroactive |
| Paid member retained through Month 2 | $2/member, split equally |
| Paid member retained Month 3+ | $1/month/member, split equally |
| Mod-produced lesson (owner approved) | $20-30 flat |
| Mini lesson / quick tip | $10 flat |
| Downloadable resource | $15 flat |
| Full course (5+ lessons) | $75-100 flat |
| Live session / AMA | $15 per session |
| Approved growth idea implemented | $5 per idea |
| Year-end loyalty (Month 12) | $200 one-time |

### Activity Minimums (must hit all 3 to qualify for bonuses that month)
1. 5 posts/replies per week (tracked in School.com dashboard)
2. 3 out of 4 weekly brainstorm calls per month
3. Reply to every new member intro within 48 hours

### Content Ownership (3 buckets)
- **Bucket 1 - Co-produced:** Owner commissioned or collaborated. Owner owns 100% IP. Mod earns flat bonus.
- **Bucket 2 - Mod independent content sold in community:** Mod owns 100%. Default split: 70% mod / 30% school.com.
- **Bucket 3 - Gray area:** Per-item split agreement signed before publishing. Default if no agreement within 7 days: 70/30. Content cannot go live without signed split agreement.

### Non-Poaching Rules
- Mods CAN open their own community on any topic
- Mods CANNOT (during agreement and 12 months after leaving):
  - Directly invite school.com members to a competing community
  - Promote a competing community inside school.com
  - DM school.com members to migrate
  - Republish content produced for school.com
- "Competing community" = substantially same topic and target audience as school.com

## 6. COMPETITIVE CONTEXT & ACQUISITION STRATEGY

- AI/automation is crowded at the generic level
- Specialization is the differentiator - "AI for CTOs and technical founders" has no direct premium competitor
- The $335K/month communities are not pure membership - they stack: membership + high-ticket coaching ($5K-25K) + courses + launches
- Small communities (<1K members) achieve 10x higher engagement than large ones
- Hormozi principle applied: premium price ($250) signals exclusivity, attracts higher-quality members, reduces churn, and makes the CTO funnel more credible
- The community is not competing with big AI communities - it is what happens after someone graduates from them

### The Graduation Model (Primary Acquisition Channel)

Our primary user acquisition is NOT Google Ads, YouTube Ads, or paid social. It is graduating members from other School communities.

**How it works:** Most AI/automation communities on School serve beginners and intermediates - they teach prompts, basic automations, "what to automate." That's valuable but shallow. When members outgrow that level and need real-world production systems - big, scalable, secure applications - they need us.

**Moderator role in acquisition:**
- All moderators must be active members of at least 3 free AI/automation communities on School
- Weekly 20-minute competitor review and strategy session with Owner and mods
- Identify members who are outgrowing their current community (asking production, scaling, or security questions nobody can answer)
- Engage authentically, provide value, and let potential graduates know school.com exists
- No spam, no mass DMs, no badmouthing other communities

**Community Organizer Referral Program:**
- Partner with organizers of other AI/automation communities
- They receive a significant referral bonus for each member who graduates to school.com's paid tier
- Win-win: organizer monetizes members they can't serve at depth, member gets production-level support
- Specific bonus amounts set by Owner before outreach begins

## 7. STRATEGIES CONSIDERED AND REJECTED

### Rejected: Low price membership ($29-49/month)
**Why rejected:** Wrong audience. At $29/month you attract hobbyists and lurkers, not technical founders with budgets. Mod income math doesn't work (need 130+ members for $5K MRR vs 20 at $250). High churn - easy to cancel low-commitment subscriptions. Contradicts Hormozi's core principle: low price signals low value to exactly the audience you want to attract.

### Rejected: Large paid community (50-100+ members)
**Why rejected:** Owner's stated cognitive limit is 20 entrepreneurs. Holding context for each member's product and situation is the core value prop. Scaling beyond 20 destroys the quality of the CTO funnel and the premium feel. Revenue upside from more members is marginal compared to CTO package revenue.

### Rejected: Base retainer for moderators (paying mods from owner's pocket before revenue)
**Why rejected:** Owner explicitly does not want to pay out of pocket. Community must fund mod compensation. Phase 1 uses non-cash perks instead. Cash only activates once community revenue supports it. This is why the 70/30 MRR split model was chosen over a fixed retainer model.

### Rejected: Individual mod performance tracking for retention bonuses
**Why rejected:** Cannot reliably attribute which mod retained which member. Creates internal competition and disputes. Equal split of retention pool keeps it clean, fair, and low-drama. Bonuses are tied to community-wide outcomes, not individual attribution.

### Rejected: Mod referral commissions as primary incentive
**Why rejected:** Mods are not content creators with audiences. They don't have communities of their own to refer from. Referral links are available but not the primary incentive. Replaced with engagement-based bonuses (posts, calls, content) and MRR share tied to community growth.

### Rejected: $199/month pricing
**Why rejected:** Requires 26 paid members to match $5K MRR (harder than 20 at $250 when capped at 20). Marginal benefit over $250 in an AI/automation professional niche where $250 still underprices the value. $250 chosen as the sweet spot: aspirational enough to signal quality, accessible enough for early traction.

### Rejected: $99-149/month pricing
**Why rejected:** See low price rejection above. At $99 you need 50+ members for meaningful MRR - impossible at 20-member cap. Ed's income from community alone never becomes meaningful. CTO funnel credibility suffers.

### Rejected: Splitting CTO retainer revenue with mods
**Why rejected:** Mods do not contribute to CTO work. Owner does all client work himself. Including CTO revenue in mod split would be paying for work they didn't do and creates perverse incentives. Explicitly excluded in moderator agreement Section 6.

### Rejected: More than 5 CTO packages
**Why rejected:** At 20+ hours for a Full Fractional client and 10 hours for Embedded, the math limits total capacity to roughly 40-50 billable hours/month while maintaining quality. 5 packages is the stated maximum. Beyond that, quality degrades and the community suffers from owner inattention.

### Rejected: Competing directly with large AI communities
**Why rejected:** Saturated market. Generic "AI tools" communities have millions of members already. Competing on volume requires massive ad spend, a large content team, and years of SEO. Positioning above them (premium, practitioner-led, small, curated) is both more achievable and more aligned with owner's CTO funnel goal.

### Rejected: Growing the free community without a paid tier for first 3+ months
**Why rejected:** Delays mod cash compensation, which hurts mod motivation. Also delays CTO funnel activation - paid members are higher-intent and more likely to become CTO clients. Paid community opens Month 2, not Month 6+.
