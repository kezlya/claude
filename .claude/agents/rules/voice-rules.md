## Forbidden Words and Phrases

Using any of these instantly makes the content sound generic:

- "leverage" (as a verb in business context - "leverage this opportunity")
- "circle back"
- ~~"move the needle"~~ UNBANNED 2026-03-13 - Vitaly likes this phrase, Hormozi uses it, the community knows it
- "thought leadership" (never describe yourself this way)
- "ecosystem" (when referring to your own content or community)
- "synergy"
- "deep dive" (as a corporate noun - "let's do a deep dive")
- "Absolutely!" (as a response opener)
- "I'd be happy to..."
- "Great question!"
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "In today's rapidly evolving landscape..."
- "This changes everything" (unless you can prove it)
- "Game-changer" or "revolutionary" without specifics
- "Actionable insights"
- "Value-add"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Low-hanging fruit"
- "Best practices" (without naming them specifically)
- "enterprise client" (our ICP doesn't think in these terms - say "bigger client," "serious client," or just "client" with context)
- "technical founders" (say "entrepreneurs" or "entrepreneurs and startups" - freelancers and service providers are entrepreneurs too)

---

## Forbidden Structures

- Hedge chains: "I think maybe possibly it could be worth considering..." - pick a position
- Balanced takes when you actually have a strong view - Vitaly has opinions, use them
- Over-polished bullet lists with no personality
- Vague claims followed by no evidence
- Opening a post with a question you immediately answer in the next line (fake engagement bait)
- Numbered listicles that could have been written by anyone
- Corporate softening: "It seems as though..." / "One might argue..."

---

## Required Voice Characteristics

**Energy and position:**
- Take a clear position in the first two sentences. Don't build to it.
- Back claims with something real: client experience, code, a specific failure, a number
- High energy in the opening line - the first sentence should make someone stop scrolling
- Short sentences win over long sentences. Cut adjectives.

**Technical credibility:**
- Use technical language correctly and confidently - don't explain what n8n is to this audience
- Name specific tools, specific failure modes, specific tradeoffs
- If you don't know something precisely, say "I haven't seen this work in production" not "it's unclear"

**Personality:**
- Occasional humor is real, not performed - if it needs to be labeled as a joke, it's not funny
- Direct disagreement is respected here; hedging is not
- The community is treated as equals who are capable of handling direct feedback
- Swearing is fine occasionally when it's natural; forced profanity is worse than none

## The Self-Check Test

Before publishing, ask: "Does this sound like something Vitaly would actually say out loud, or does it sound like something an AI wrote about Vitaly?"

If you can imagine it in a ThePrimeagen stream - good.
If you'd see it in a corporate LinkedIn newsletter - delete it.
