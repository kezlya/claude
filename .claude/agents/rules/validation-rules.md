# Validation Rules - Wishful Thinking and Reality Check Patterns

This file is the institutional memory of every time Vitaly's thinking was checked against reality. It grows over time.

---

## The Two Goals (Always Check Against These)

Every idea, decision, or change must be evaluated against one or both of these:

1. **CTO Retainer Pipeline:** Does this generate or advance 2+ active CTO retainer clients? ($4K-$10K/month each)
2. **Paid Cap:** Does this fill or maintain the paid tier at/near 20 members? ($250/month x 20 = $5K MRR)

If an idea doesn't contribute to either goal, it needs a very strong argument to justify the time investment.

## Seed Patterns - Common Wishful Thinking in Community Building

These are the traps that kill community businesses. Every one of them feels reasonable in the moment.

**Trap 1: "Build it and they will come"**
Creating content, channels, or features before validating that anyone wants them. Reality: the first question is always "who specifically is waiting for this and why?"

**Trap 2: "Engagement equals intent"**
Likes, comments, emoji reactions, and even substantive discussion are NOT indicators of CTO retainer interest. People engage with content they find interesting. They hire people who solve their specific problem.

**Trap 3: "More is better"**
More channels, more topics, more content, more features. Reality: adding more to a community that hasn't proved its core value is a distraction. Hormozi principle: get one thing working before layering on complexity.

**Trap 4: "Vocal members represent everyone"**
The loudest voices in a community rarely represent the actual membership. The lurkers and the quiet members are often the ones with budget.

**Trap 5: "Nice words mean pipeline"**
"This community is amazing!" / "You're so knowledgeable!" - these are signals of a good experience, not signals of a buying intent. Flattery is cheap. Budget is expensive.

**Trap 6: "This feature will fix retention"**
Retention comes from value delivered on the member's specific problem. Features don't retain people; outcomes do.

**Trap 7: "We need scale before monetization"**
Waiting for 500 members before testing CTO retainer conversion is backwards. 20 high-signal members are more valuable than 500 lurkers.

**Trap 8: "The content is great, we just need distribution"**
Usually the content needs work. Distribution problems are often content problems in disguise.

**Trap 9: "This worked in another community"**
Context is everything. A tactic that works for a general-interest community doesn't automatically work for a premium, ICP-specific community with a different revenue model.

**Trap 10: "If I do more, things will improve"**
Activity is not progress. The question is always: which specific action creates the next CTO retainer lead or paid member?

## Scoring Anchors

Use these to calibrate scores:

**Wishful Thinking Score:**
- 1-3: Evidence-based, minimal assumptions
- 4-6: Some assumptions, some evidence - needs clarification
- 7-10: More belief than evidence - high risk of wasted effort

**Reality Check Score:**
- 7-10: Evidence exists that this works in comparable contexts
- 4-6: Plausible but unproven - test before committing fully
- 1-3: Contradicts known patterns - strong case needed to proceed

**80/20 Score:**
- 7-10: Directly attacks the constraint (CTO pipeline or paid cap), minimal effort
- 4-6: Indirect contribution, moderate effort
- 1-3: Marginal impact or high effort relative to outcome

**Goal Proximity Score:**
- 7-10: Clear, direct path to CTO retainer lead or paid member conversion
- 4-6: Indirect contribution - might help, maybe
- 1-3: No clear connection to either goal

---

## Verdict Thresholds

- **GO:** Reality >= 7 AND Goal Proximity >= 6 AND 80/20 >= 6
- **REVISIT:** Any key score between 4-6, or conflicting scores
- **NO-GO:** Reality <= 3, OR Wishful Thinking >= 7, OR Goal Proximity <= 2
