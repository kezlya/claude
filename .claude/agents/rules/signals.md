# Signal Classification Rules

How to tell the difference between a community member who is a CTO retainer prospect and someone who is just passing through.

The core principle: members come to fix pain. They don't come to socialize. Someone asking a real production question is more interesting than someone who just left 10 likes.

**Always read `playbook/icp.md` before classifying.** The ICP defines who we're looking for. The key filter is cost-of-failure: does this person run a business where downtime or errors directly cost them clients or revenue? Semi-technical skill alone is not enough - freelancers billing by the hour rarely convert to retainer. Business owners with real stakes do.

---

## Hot Signal - Act Immediately

Flag to Vitaly for direct follow-up (DM or private note). These are buying signals.

Indicators:
- Describes a production problem with real business stakes ("we're losing customers because...", "this keeps breaking in prod...", "our team can't figure out...")
- Mentions budget pressure, timeline pressure, or team limitations ("we can't afford to hire a full-time...", "need this done before our launch...")
- Asks about something that requires ongoing expertise, not a one-time answer (architecture decisions, scaling strategy, security review, hiring technical talent)
- Their posts demonstrate a real system: mentions scale numbers, team size, revenue context, or specific technical depth (not "I'm building a chatbot" but "we're at 50k API calls/day and hitting rate limits")
- Explicitly asks if Vitaly does private work, advisory, or consulting
- Posts a problem where a community comment cannot fully solve it - needs a full technical engagement
- Non-technical founder asking about transitioning from no-code to production code with budget + urgency signals

**Recommended action:** DM directly or flag for follow-up within 24 hours.

---

## Warm Signal - Monitor and Engage Publicly

Not ready to pitch. Worth investing in public engagement to build the relationship.

Indicators:
- Replies to Vitaly's posts with substantive technical additions (not just "great point!")
- Asks follow-up questions specifically about Vitaly's content or approach
- Tags Vitaly in relevant technical discussions
- Has been consistently active for 2+ weeks
- Demonstrates technical seniority in their own posts (they know what they're talking about)
- Asks questions that show they're actually building something real

**Recommended action:** Respond publicly with depth. Make the public interaction high-value. They're watching to see how Vitaly handles real problems.

---

## Noise - Socially Fine, Not Pipeline

Engage lightly or ignore. Don't read into it.

Indicators:
- Generic "great post!" reactions or emoji-only responses
- Questions with easy answers (Googleable in under 2 minutes)
- Off-topic posts (general AI hype, politics, personal life)
- One-time activity with no follow-up
- Motivation-seeking: "should I even start building with AI?" / "is this worth learning?"
- Posts about general industry trends without a specific problem
- Members who only post wins but never post problems (they may not need help)
- Lurkers with no posted content after 2+ weeks

**Recommended action:** Light social response if any. No pipeline follow-up.

---

## Special Case: Paid Tier Conversion Signal

Someone in the free tier who is warm-to-hot and has been around 3+ weeks. Look for:
- Multiple substantive posts across different topics
- Increasing engagement depth over time (not one-and-done)
- A problem that the free community is helping with but not fully solving

**Recommended action:** DM with a specific offer: "Based on what you've been working on, the paid tier would give you X - want to talk about it?"
