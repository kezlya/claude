## Section 2: Community Vision

### Q: What specific transformation do you want members to achieve after 90 days?
**A:** "Ship product MVP or automate business saving 100h/month"

### Q: What's your primary monetization model?
**A:** "I feel like we need two communities: one free one and one paid one. The paid one will be a little bit more serious, result-oriented. And the free one will be more of a fun side. But I want better people. I want to reach people who know me for a few months in the free community and who are happy and can afford easily to join me in the paid one and get more VIP attention."

### Q: What's your financial goal for this community in 12 months?
**A:** "I want to receive from this community at least $10,000 monthly. Let's aim for this."

### Q: What's the minimum monthly revenue that would make the community worth continuing after 6 months?
**A:** "$2,000/month"

## Section 3: Target Audience

### Q: Who is your ideal member?
**A:** "For the client—the ideal member for me, the client who will pay and whom I can bring the most value—it's a non-technical entrepreneur. But for the community of engineers and developers, my people (let's put it this way, not my clients, but my people), it's the frustrated builder. I am a person who loves to build, who can build, who knows the pain, but who is not quite at my level, so my experience can be very valuable for that person."

### Q: US vs Russian market?
**A:** "US primary, Russia secondary. I don't want to do double work. Most likely I need like a few hundred rich kids who can support the community, and then when they support me, I can support a hundred not rich kids, but very talented. So develop real diamonds from them in terms of talent, not money."

### Q: How should both audiences coexist?
**A:** "Let's mix them for now. I'm totally okay inside of community. The builders will help non-technical people, so the landing page can welcome both of them, and position that they will find each other. That's totally fine under my supervision."

## Section 4: Pricing & Monetization

### Q: What price point for paid tier?
**A:** "I would prefer if they were clients who have a lot of money and who can pay $500 per month, no problem at all. Like they enjoy doing it and consulting with me in a paid community."

### Q: What justifies $500/month?
**A:** "Direct access to me, done-with-you builds, strategic sessions and brainstorm with them, playing a role as a fractional CTO. They will get, let's say, two hours of me per month or three hours. If they hire me directly, that will be $750, but if they hire me through this community, that's only $500 per month."

### Q: When to introduce paid tier?
**A:** "When 5 people ask"

### Q: Who pays for paid tier in mixed community?
**A:** "The paid community or features will be access to me where I personally involved"

## Section 5: Content & Engagement

### Q: What content format are you most comfortable creating?
**A:** "My cup of tea is live sessions. That's probably the easiest for me. If I have questions, I would like to answer them. Then video walkthroughs, I can review someone's code, give comments. But writing tutorials is kind of annoying for me, so it's not my cup of tea."

### Q: What's your weekly time commitment?
**A:** "In January and February, it will most likely be 5 to 10 hours per week, but in a sprint I can bring it up a little bit. I can hold one live session per week for sure."

### Q: If you could only do ONE thing weekly, what would it be?
**A:** "1 hot seat review"

### Q: How long and how to select hot seats?
**A:** "30 min, most engaged - reward active members with hot seat priority"

### Q: How should hot seat content be repurposed?
**A:** "I want every session to be somehow converted into case studies or more advertising. Record a session, submit it into my n8n pipeline, and a couple outputs were produced as a LinkedIn article, maybe YouTube short or YouTube video, maybe a couple Twitter or X posts."

### Q: Do you expect implementation follow-up from hot seat recipients?
**A:** "Encourage but optional, or public progress tracking is probably better for the community so everyone can see how that product grows."

## Section 6: Community Principles

### Q: What are your non-negotiable community principles?
**A:**
1. Ship over perfection - Bias toward action, done > perfect
2. No politics/drama - Focus on building, no culture war debates
3. Quality matters - Fast doesn't mean sloppy - enterprise-grade thinking

### Q: What kind of members do you NOT want?
**A:** "I don't want sassy people. I don't want annoying people. I don't want snowflakes. I don't want people who just talk about politics and stuff or complain all the time. I want doers. I want exciting people, entrepreneurs who are ready to build with their hands or with the hands of others, but ready to create something new in this world."

### Q: How to filter for bad fits?
**A:** "Scenario questions - 'You disagree with advice given - what do you do?'"

## Section 7: Community Structure

### Q: Landing page primary CTA - staged approach?
**A:** "Before I have 20 members, I want them to book a call so I can talk to them directly. After 20 members until 100 members, apply to join. I want them to join a waitlist where I will let them in if I decide that their answer is good and they will be a viable community member."

### Q: Member roles for self-sustaining community?
**A:** "I can bring my employees as senior members to answer questions and serve as topic experts. Eventually, we can switch into weekly champions or peer accountability. In the beginning, to kickstart all this, I have topic experts and I have senior members answering."

### Q: What happens when you're unavailable?
**A:** "Pre-recorded content - pre-record a couple of sessions when I build products and just keep them for days like this or weeks like this. Also community-led sessions."

### Q: Team involvement?
**A:** "Co-moderators. Right now, since this is a free community, it will be part of their job. When I start making money from this community, I can definitely arrange a bonus or commissions."

When you build your products with agents and AI, there is a point when things start to be very fragile and break and fall apart. So I'm the guy who can facilitate transition from that into enterprise-grade products. I work in the enterprise, I build enterprise, I lead multiple startups from A to enterprise level, including passing compliance. I know both worlds very deeply, very well. And I know how to transition and, most importantly, when to transition from one to another."

### Q: What makes YOU the right person to run this community?
**A:** "My track record on both sides of the enterprise software, 20 years of experience in tech, and my knowledge in AI and agents."

### Q: Do you have a framework/methodology for agent orchestration?
**A:** "Developing it. I would like to develop them publicly, teach to learn and develop methodology publicly through hot seats and sessions. I would like to collaborate with members and listen to their inputs. I already have some pieces."
