## 1. What We Are Building Together

The Community consists of two tiers:

- **Free tier** - organic growth funnel, open to all
- **Paid tier** - $250/month, capped at 20 members, premium access to Vitaly

The Moderator helps keep both tiers healthy, growing, and valuable for members. The Owner handles paid advertising ($800/month budget), overall strategy, technical leadership, and final approval on all content and decisions.

**What the Owner brings:** Technical leadership, 20+ years of engineering experience, organizational structure, direction, and the brand that attracts members.

**What the Moderator brings:** Time, presence, and the ability to make members feel at home. Moderators are the people who talk to members, welcome them, keep conversations going, and provide expertise in their areas when the Owner is not available.

## 2. Three Phases - Year 1 Roadmap

### Phase 1: Months 1-3 - Founding Period (Pre-Revenue)

No cash payments yet. In return for your time, you receive:

- Free lifetime access to the paid community (permanently yours, even if you later leave)
- Founding Moderator status - a permanent title recognizing your early contribution
- Priority placement in the community services marketplace
- 0% commission on any services you sell inside the Community during this phase

Milestone bonuses below are earned now but paid out once the paid community launches in Phase 2.

### Phase 2: Months 4-6 - Launch Period

Cash bonuses activate once the paid community reaches 20 paying members.

Revenue share structure:
- 30% of community MRR goes to the moderator pool
- Moderator pool is split equally among all active moderators (3 moderators = 10% each)
- At full capacity (20 members x $250 = $5,000 MRR), each moderator receives $500/month from the MRR share

### Phase 3: Months 7-12 - Growth Period

Same structure as Phase 2 with improved rates as a reward for staying the full year. Specific rates agreed upon together at Month 6 review.

## 3. How You Earn - Bonus Structure

All bonuses in this section go into a **moderator bonus pool** and are split equally among all active moderators that month. This keeps the Owner's costs predictable regardless of how many moderators are active, and ensures moderators are never penalized for the team growing.

### Free Community Growth Bonuses (Phase 1 earned / Phase 2+ paid)

| Milestone | Bonus Pool (split among active mods) |
|---|---|
| Free community reaches 100 members | $200 pool (retroactive, paid in Phase 2) |
| Free community reaches 250 members | $500 pool (retroactive, paid in Phase 2) |
| Free community reaches 500 members | $1,000 pool (retroactive, paid in Phase 2) |
| Monthly growth of +50 members (Phase 2+) | $100 pool |
| Monthly growth of +100 members (Phase 2+) | $200 pool |

### Paid Community Retention Bonuses (Phase 2+)

| Trigger | Bonus Pool (split among active mods) |
|---|---|
| Member retained through Month 2 | $20 per retained member into pool |
| Member retained through Month 3+ | $10/month per retained member into pool |

Retention bonuses reflect the premium nature of the paid tier - each member pays $250/month. Retaining one member for one extra month generates $250 in revenue, so this bonus structure stays within ~8% of that revenue while still being a meaningful incentive.

Tracking is done by join date in a shared Google Sheet reviewed together monthly.

### Content Creation

There are two ways moderators can be involved with content:

**1. Owner-produced content, moderator assists**

When the Owner creates content (lessons, courses, videos) and a moderator helps - editing, formatting, research, follow-up materials, etc. - the Owner owns 100% of the IP and 100% of ongoing revenue. The moderator receives a one-time flat fee for their contribution, agreed before the work begins. This keeps costs scoped and predictable for both sides.

**2. Moderator-produced content sold in the Community**

When a moderator independently creates and produces content (lessons, templates, courses, resources), they own 100% of the IP. Owner approval is required before publishing - no approval means no listing. Revenue split by phase:

- **Phase 1:** 100% to Moderator (0% commission, as stated in Section 2)
- **Phase 2+:** 90% Moderator / 10% Community

This is the same split as marketplace services (Section 8). The Community provides the audience; the 10% platform cut in Phase 2+ reflects that value.

**Live sessions and AMAs**

Moderators who run live sessions or AMAs inside the Community receive $100 per session (flat, paid by Owner). Live sessions are the strongest retention driver in premium communities and are compensated directly.

**Growth ideas**

Moderators are encouraged to propose growth ideas. There is no separate bonus for ideas - if an idea works, the moderator benefits through the growth bonuses in the pool above.

### Loyalty Bonus

Any moderator completing the full 12 months receives a one-time $500 bonus and first right to renew with improved terms.

## 4. Minimum Activity Requirements

Bonus eligibility requires meeting ALL three minimums each month. Missing a month removes eligibility for that month only - not future months.

| Requirement | Minimum Bar |
|---|---|
| Posts in community | 3 per week (tracked in the Community dashboard) |
| Replies in community | 5 per day (tracked in the Community dashboard) |

Why we track this: Vague expectations create resentment. These are binary - you either hit them or you don't. There is no arguing over perception. Skool.com's dashboard records all activity automatically.

### Optional Bonus Activities

These are not required but earn additional bonuses when completed: running live sessions, creating pinned resources, recruiting a quality moderator ($25 one-time), or producing approved independent content.

## 5. Competitor Intelligence & Community Scouting

### Weekly Competitor Review (Required)

Each moderator must actively participate in at least 3 free AI/automation communities on Skool.com (or equivalent platforms). This is a core moderator responsibility, not optional.

**What this involves:**
- Join and stay active in free-tier AI/automation communities that are shallow in execution - the "prompt dump" and "what to automate" type communities
- Once per week, participate in a 20-minute competitor review and strategy session with the Owner and other moderators
- Come prepared with observations: what's working in other communities, what's missing, what members are complaining about, what topics are underserved

**Why this matters:** Our positioning is "what happens after you graduate from those communities." You can't position against something you don't understand. You need to know what members are learning elsewhere so you can speak to the gap between shallow AI automation and real-world production systems.

**What to look for:**
- Members who are clearly outgrowing the community (asking production questions, scaling questions, security questions that nobody can answer)
- Content gaps - topics that are never covered because the community owner lacks depth
- Common frustrations and unmet needs
- Community structure and engagement patterns we can learn from or improve on

## 6. Member Acquisition - The Graduation Model

### How We Grow

Our primary acquisition channel is NOT paid ads on Google or YouTube. It is graduating members from other Skool.com communities.

**The concept:** Most AI/automation communities on Skool.com serve beginners and intermediates. They teach prompts, basic automations, and "what to automate." That's valuable - but it has a ceiling. When members outgrow that level and need real-world production systems - big, scalable, secure applications - they need us. They've "graduated" from those communities.

**How moderators contribute to this:**
- Through your presence in other communities (Section 5), you naturally encounter members who are ready for the next level
- You do NOT poach or spam. You engage authentically, provide value, and when someone clearly needs production-grade support, you let them know the Community exists
- You flag potential "graduates" to the Owner during the weekly competitor review session

### Community Organizer Referral Program

We will develop referral partnerships with organizers of other AI/automation communities:
- Other community organizers receive a significant referral bonus for each member who graduates from their community and joins the Community's paid tier
- This is a win-win: the organizer monetizes members they can no longer serve at depth, and the member gets the production-level support they need
- Moderators help identify and build relationships with organizers of communities where this partnership makes sense
- Specific referral bonus amounts will be set by the Owner and shared with moderators before outreach begins

**What is NOT allowed:**
- Mass DMing members of other communities
- Posting promotional content in other communities without organizer permission
- Badmouthing other communities to recruit their members
- Any behavior that would get the Community banned from other platforms

## 9. Fractional CTO Revenue - Exclusion

The Owner operates Fractional CTO retainer packages (Advisory at $4,000/month, Embedded at $7,000/month, Full Fractional at $10,000/month) as a separate business line. Community members are the primary lead source for these packages.

**All Fractional CTO retainer revenue is 100% to the Owner and 0% to moderators.** The Owner does all CTO client work personally. Moderators do not contribute to CTO engagements and do not share in CTO revenue. This exclusion applies regardless of whether the CTO client was originally a community member or was introduced by a moderator.

This is explicitly stated here to prevent any future ambiguity.

## 10. Non-Poaching - Protecting the Partnership

This section exists not because of distrust, but because the friendship matters more than any community. Defining this upfront removes any future ambiguity.

**What is fully allowed:**
- Opening your own Skool.com community on any topic
- Selling your own content and services anywhere outside the Community
- Leaving this agreement with 30 days written notice

**What is not allowed** (during the agreement and for 12 months after leaving):
- Directly inviting the Community members to a competing community
- Promoting a competing community inside the Community while serving as moderator
- DMing the Community members to migrate to another platform
- Republishing content produced for the Community under your own brand

A "competing community" means a community with substantially the same topic and target audience as the Community. You can build whatever you want. Just not a direct copy pointed at our members.

## 11. Exit Terms

Either party may exit with 30 days written notice. Upon exit:
- All earned but unpaid bonuses are paid within 30 days
- Unearned milestone bonuses are forfeited
- Founding Moderator title and lifetime community access are retained permanently
- The 12-month non-poaching clause (Section 10) applies from the exit date
